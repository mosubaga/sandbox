The Method

Poirot’s cases are not about darkness creeping in—they’re about light becoming focused. Each clue is placed with care, every contradiction is measured, and the solution arrives with calm certainty: the work of the little grey cells.

Little Grey Cells: Start with order. Arrange the timeline. Test each alibi. When the facts line up, the guilty party stands out like a stain on ivory.

Signature Atmosphere

  • Setting: salons, hotels, trains, country houses—civilized spaces with hidden motives.
  • Tone: elegant, controlled tension—no chaos, only concealed truth.
  • Visual: brass, geometry, clean borders, deliberate symmetry.
  • Reveal: the drawing-room conclusion—facts aligned, culprit exposed.
